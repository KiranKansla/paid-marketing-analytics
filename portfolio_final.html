Project Context

The Business Problem

A large B2B organization was running paid marketing across multiple Google Ads accounts, markets, and business areas — each managed independently. Over time they had accumulated 5,000+ performance records with no unified view of portfolio efficiency or data quality available to leadership.

I was engaged to consolidate this large-scale, decentralized data, build a meaningful KPI framework, and surface what was really happening across the portfolio — communicated clearly enough for non-technical senior stakeholders to act on immediately.

📋 Scope of Engagement

5,000+ records reviewed and cleaned · Multi-currency normalization (EUR, GBP → SEK) · Consolidated Power BI dashboard · Business insight extraction · 1-page executive stakeholder summary

Data Quality

What I Found Before Analysis Even Started

Real-world enterprise data is never clean. I documented four structural issues upfront — because transparent limitations are more valuable than overconfident analysis built on flawed data.

Multi-Currency Reporting

Accounts reported in EUR, GBP, and SEK. Normalized everything to SEK using proxy exchange rates. Values are directionally accurate for comparison but may not reflect exact financials.

Inconsistent Naming Conventions

Account and campaign names were non-standardized across the portfolio — a common symptom of decentralized management. Required cleaning before reliable aggregation was possible.

No Revenue Data Available

Conversion value data was absent across the dataset. Analysis focuses on volume and efficiency metrics. ROI and ROAS analysis was flagged as a data gap — not estimated.

Conversion Definition Variance

Conversion events may be defined differently across accounts. Similar counts may represent different user actions. All cross-account CVR comparisons noted this caveat explicitly.

Key Findings

What The Data Actually Showed

🔴 Critical Finding — Netherlands Account

Google Ads Trucks Netherlands: 1.16M kr spend · 87,812 clicks · only 526 conversions · CVR 0.60% · CPA 2,206 kr. Portfolio average CPA: 56.68 kr. The Netherlands account is running at 39× the portfolio CPA. Traffic is arriving but not converting — this is a post-click problem. Likely causes: landing page friction or audience mismatch, not ad creative.

⭐ Benchmark Finding — Construction Equipment

Construction Equipment account: CVR 21.62% · CPA 49.88 kr — the strongest performer in the portfolio. This account's strategy should be studied and replicated. Its budget should be scaled, not the underperformers.

Methodology

How I Approached The Analysis

1

Data Review & Quality Assessment

Reviewed 5,000+ records across accounts, markets, and time periods. Documented all structural data quality issues before touching a single formula — multi-currency reporting, inconsistent naming, missing revenue data, and conversion definition variance.

2

Normalization & Data Modeling

Standardized all spend figures to SEK using proxy exchange rates. Resolved naming inconsistencies across accounts. Built a dimensional data model in Power BI with fact and dimension table structure.

3

KPI Framework — Full Funnel

Designed a four-stage KPI framework: CTR (awareness efficiency) → CPC (traffic cost) → CVR (post-click quality) → CPA (acquisition efficiency). Each metric diagnoses a different funnel stage so problems are located precisely, not just flagged.

4

Power BI Dashboard — 8 Visuals

Built CPA by account (horizontal bar, color-coded), spend by market (top 5), CVR by campaign type, conversion funnel, spend vs conversions scatter plot, monthly trend combo chart, and a red alert banner. All color coding is data-driven via DAX — no manual formatting.

5

Stakeholder Communication

Delivered a 1-page executive summary structured for senior non-technical stakeholders: Key Findings → Data Caveats → Next Steps. Designed to be actionable in 60 seconds, not explored over 30 minutes.

DAX Measures Built

-- Portfolio KPIs
CPA = DIVIDE(SUM([Cost In SEK]), SUM([Conversions]), 0)
CVR = DIVIDE(SUM([Conversions]), SUM([Clicks]), 0)
CTR = DIVIDE(SUM([Clicks]), SUM([Impr.]), 0)
CPC = DIVIDE(SUM([Cost In SEK]), SUM([Clicks]), 0)

-- Spend share — data-driven % of total
Spend % of Total = DIVIDE(SUM([Cost In SEK]),
  CALCULATE(SUM([Cost In SEK]), ALLSELECTED([Country/Territory])), 0)

-- Dynamic color — no hardcoding, data drives the color
CPA Color = IF([CPA] > 500, "#E24B4A",
  IF([CPA] < 50, "#639922", "#378ADD"))

Recommendations

What I Recommended To Leadership

🔴 Immediate — Audit Netherlands

Pause or significantly reduce NL Trucks spend. Audit landing page and audience targeting. 87K clicks with only 526 conversions (0.60% CVR) is a post-click breakdown — the ad is working, the destination isn't.

🟢 Immediate — Scale Construction Eq.

Redirect budget from Netherlands to Construction Equipment. At 49.88 kr CPA and 21.62% CVR, this account is proven. Additional spend here generates more return per krona than any other account.

📊 Strategic — Live Reporting

Replace ad-hoc analysis with an automated live dashboard with daily FX normalization. Leadership should see this data in real time — not months after the inefficiency developed.

📋 Strategic — Standardize Data

Align conversion definitions and naming conventions across all accounts. Current inconsistency creates reporting blind spots and limits the reliability of cross-account comparison.

📌 Additional Data Needed For Deeper Analysis

Conversion value / revenue data (ROAS calculation) · Customer segmentation / audience data · Budget allocation targets by account · Historical market benchmarks
